Red Custard Apple

Annona reticulata

Known for its “sweet & cream raspberry cheesecake” flavor, this tropical fruit is beloved for its custard-like texture and aromatic sweetness.

Botanical Details
  • Family: Annonaceae
  • Native to Central America
  • Medium-sized tree with spreading canopy
  • Fruit has a reticulated (net-like) skin pattern
Identification Tips
  • Heart-shaped or rounded fruit
  • Soft, creamy interior
  • Sweet aroma when ripe

Black Sapote

Often called the “chocolate pudding fruit,” Black Sapote is famous for its rich, smooth, cocoa-like flavor.

Botanical Details
  • Family: Ebenaceae
  • Native to Mexico & Central America
  • Evergreen tree with glossy leaves
  • Fruit turns dark brown/black when ripe

Star Apple / Caimito

Described as having a “condensed milk taste,” this fruit is creamy, sweet, and visually stunning when cut open.

Botanical Details
  • Family: Sapotaceae
  • Native to the Caribbean & Central America
  • Latex present in skin and leaves
  • Leaves have a metallic bronze underside

Roxburgh Fig

“Mildly sweet, intense berry aroma, raspberry taste without tartness.” A rare fig from the depths of the Indian jungle.

Botanical Details
  • Family: Moraceae
  • Native to India & Southeast Asia
  • Large leaves with rough texture
  • Fruit grows in clusters on trunk (cauliflorous)

Cecropia peltata

Known as the “gummy worm fruit,” with a flavor reminiscent of fig & kiwi.

Botanical Details
  • Family: Urticaceae
  • Native to tropical Americas
  • Fast-growing pioneer species
  • Hollow stems often inhabited by ants
